Code Snippet

void setup() {
   //Initializes digital pin LED_BUILTIN as output
   pinMode(11, OUTPUT); //Sets output to port 11
   pinMode(10, OUTPUT); //Sets output to port 10
   pinMode(9, OUTPUT); //Sets output to port 9
}
void loop() {
   // loop
   digitalWrite(11, HIGH); //Sets the voltage level to HIGH and turns on LED
   delay(500); //Sets delay to 500 milliseconds
   digitalWrite(9, LOW); //Sets the voltage level to LOW and turns off LED
   delay(500); //Sets delay to 500 milliseconds
   digitalWrite(10, HIGH); //Sets the voltage level to HIGH and turns on LED
   delay(500); //Sets delay to 500 milliseconds
   digitalWrite(9, LOW); //Sets the voltage level to LOW and turns off LED
   delay(500); //Sets delay to 500 milliseconds
   digitalWrite(9, HIGH); //Sets the voltage level to HIGH and turns on LED
   delay(500); //Sets delay to 500 milliseconds
   digitalWrite(9, LOW); //Sets the voltage level to LOW and turns off LED
   delay(500); //Sets delay to 500 milliseconds
}

This is the code snippet used to make my LED board blink. The idea was to have a loop have the LED light up and delay until the next light is commanded to turn on. This is how the blinking pattern was created.

Circuit Board

I picked the value 220 for the resistor because I used the equation 5V - 1.8V = (20*10^-3)R. 5V represents the initial voltage and 1.8 represents the voltage drop. (20*20^-3) represents the 20mA desired current.

Questions

1: The picture to the left shows the graph for question #1. 2: Arduino Uno has 14 pins so it could power 14 individual LEDs. This would draw 280 mA of current because 20mA required per LED multiplied by 14 individual LEDs gives us 280mA of current. 3: The technical answer I googled was that the flicker fusion threshold is around 60-90 Hz. Anything above this would appear to be fading in and out. The less technical answer would be around 100ms as that is what I set my Arduino delay time to and it seemed to be fading in and out rather than blinking.
